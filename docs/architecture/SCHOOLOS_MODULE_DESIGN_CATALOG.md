## 3. Cross-Module Design Rules

1. `tenantId` is the tenant boundary for modules, jobs, files, exports, reports, notifications, cache, mobile, and learning.
2. Backend authorization and module entitlement are authoritative.
3. Files follow `Feature module -> FileRegistryService -> StorageService -> StorageAdapter`.
4. Money modules use backend/database totals, idempotency, audit, and reversal/correction.
5. M12 owns notification delivery; source modules emit normalized events only.
6. M15 owns notice authoring and emits normalized publication events to M12; preview and final dispatch use the same backend recipient rules.
7. Chat/conversations are removed from the active product and are not a deferred module. New writes and active navigation are disabled; historical data, migrations, authorization, moderation evidence, and retention obligations remain protected until approved disposal.
8. M13 remains separate, deferred, and frozen. Preserve its implementation, data, migrations, contracts, clients, tests, permissions, and entitlements; keep it disabled by default for pilot tenants and do not expand it.
9. M14 is roadmap-only and must not be implemented as incidental analytics or AI inside another module.

### 3.1 Advanced operations foundations

Advanced operations deepen active M0-M12 and M15 workflows without creating another module, expanding frozen M13, or introducing AI. Current repository foundations and their required ownership are:

| Foundation                                 | Canonical ownership                                                                                              | Required behavior before release claims                                                                                             |
| ------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Approval workflows                         | M0 shared workflow infrastructure; the requesting feature module owns final business validation and application. | Tenant-scoped requests, reasoned rejection, safe before/after context, idempotent final application, module-visible status, audit.  |
| Rules-based automation                     | M0 shared automation infrastructure; feature modules emit approved triggers; M12 owns notification delivery.     | Tenant/feature enablement, deterministic conditions/actions, retry-safe execution, provider-disabled honesty, execution audit.      |
| Descriptive analytics                      | Each module owns bounded summaries; principal/platform surfaces compose them without duplicating business logic. | Backend-owned totals, as-of timestamps, permission filtering, bounded queries, no client-derived official KPIs.                     |
| Document templates and generated documents | Feature module -> File Registry -> StorageService; M0 may provide shared template infrastructure.                | Versioned templates, protected artifacts, generation actor/time, reprint/access audit, localization-ready labels.                   |
| Export jobs                                | Source module owns report semantics; shared queue/export infrastructure owns execution state.                    | Tenant-scoped queued work, bounded status, retry permission/reason, protected File Registry result, expiry/retention policy.        |
| Mobile/offline reliability                 | Purpose-limited feature APIs and Flutter feature repositories.                                                   | Safe cached reads, explicit stale/offline state, idempotent approved drafts only, conflict handling, logout/session-expiry cleanup. |

These foundations must not bypass module ownership. Approval, automation, analytics, document, or export infrastructure cannot write official fee, payroll, accounting, academic, attendance, notification-delivery, or notice-publication state without the owning module revalidating authorization, lifecycle, idempotency, and audit requirements.

### 3.3 M7 Nepal statutory verification artifact

SchoolOS does **not** currently establish Nepal payroll compliance. The runtime calculation in `apps/api/src/payroll/payroll.service.ts` applies demo-only values of 10% employee PF, 10% employer PF, and 1% TDS when the related salary-structure flags are enabled. The statutory-deduction endpoint reports those configured values, but there is no effective-dated, authority-referenced, approved statutory policy model. Every current or future statutory rule remains `NEEDS_NEPAL_COMPLIANCE_VERIFICATION` until a qualified Nepal Chartered Accountant or payroll/legal reviewer signs off on the source, applicability, calculation examples, accounting treatment, and effective dates.

| Area                                | Current repository evidence                                                                                                 | Verification status                   | Required next evidence                                                                                                                                     |
| ----------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| TDS and PAN                         | `tdsEnabled`, staff PAN, demo 1% deduction, TDS report and payable account exist.                                           | `NEEDS_NEPAL_COMPLIANCE_VERIFICATION` | Authority source, fiscal-year slabs/thresholds, annualization and adjustment rules, PAN/non-PAN treatment, examples, filing/report format, posting review. |
| PF / EPF                            | `pfEnabled`, demo 10% employee and employer calculations, PF report and payable/expense accounts exist.                     | `NEEDS_NEPAL_COMPLIANCE_VERIFICATION` | Scheme eligibility, contribution base, caps, employer/employee rates, rounding, enrollment identifiers, remittance/report rules, posting review.           |
| SSF                                 | No dedicated verified policy or calculation chain found.                                                                    | `NEEDS_NEPAL_COMPLIANCE_VERIFICATION` | Applicability versus PF/EPF, contribution base/rates/caps, employer/employee split, remittance/reporting, account treatment.                               |
| CIT                                 | Tenant salary structures may contain a named deduction component; no statutory CIT policy engine exists.                    | `NEEDS_NEPAL_COMPLIANCE_VERIFICATION` | Eligibility, limits, tax treatment, payroll-versus-voluntary deduction boundary, remittance/reporting, account treatment.                                  |
| Gratuity                            | No verified accrual, eligibility, vesting, settlement, or journal policy found.                                             | `NEEDS_NEPAL_COMPLIANCE_VERIFICATION` | Legal applicability, service thresholds, accrual/settlement formula, termination handling, liability/expense accounts.                                     |

| Leave without pay                   | Approved unpaid leave affects paid days and payroll deduction.                                                              | `NEEDS_NEPAL_COMPLIANCE_VERIFICATION` | Calendar/workday basis, partial-day rules, caps, interaction with statutory contribution bases and festival allowance.                                     |
| Employer and employee contributions | PF has demo values; other contributions may only be generic components.                                                     | `NEEDS_NEPAL_COMPLIANCE_VERIFICATION` | Per-policy contribution base, caps, rounding, taxable treatment, payable/expense mappings, corrections and retroactivity.                                  |

| Posting account treatment           | Payroll accrual uses an effective source mapping plus system PF/TDS/other-payable and employer-expense accounts.            | `NEEDS_NEPAL_COMPLIANCE_VERIFICATION` | CA-approved chart treatment for each deduction/contribution, settlement entries, reversals, period locks, reconciliation, disclosures.                     |
| School-type applicability           | No statutory rule matrix by `SCHOOL` or `HIGHER_SECONDARY` exists.                                                          | `NEEDS_NEPAL_COMPLIANCE_VERIFICATION` | Legal-employer and employee-category applicability; school stage must not change a statutory rule without authority evidence.                              |

Proposed contract-first policy model (not yet implemented; `NEEDS_SCHEMA_DESIGN`) must be tenant-scoped and include policy name, version, effective-from/to dates, authority/source reference, applicability, employee and employer contribution logic, tax treatment, thresholds/caps, M11 account mappings, approval state, verification state, and a superseded-policy reference. Draft or unverified policies must never calculate official payroll or activate posting. A future migration must preserve the exact policy version on every payroll run/line and remain replay-safe against existing runs.

Required reviewer checklist:

1. Identify the current primary authority and publication date for every statutory area; retain a protected evidence copy or approved external reference.
2. Record employee/employer applicability, contribution or tax base, slabs, thresholds, caps, rounding, minimums, exemptions, and effective dates.
3. Review worked examples for regular pay, partial period, unpaid leave, arrears/corrections, termination, festival allowance, overtime, and fiscal-year transition.
4. Approve M11 expense, payable, settlement, reversal, and correction mappings and confirm locked/closed-period treatment.
5. Approve PF/TDS/SSF/CIT reports, identifiers, remittance timing, reconciliation, retention, and protected export requirements.
6. Confirm PAN and bank-data masking, payroll permission separation, tenant isolation, audit contents, and staff self-service scope.
7. Sign the policy version and verification state. Only an approved and verified effective-dated version may be activated after migration, contract, calculation, posting, browser, and regression evidence passes.
