# SchoolOS Pilot Interview Screener

**Status:** Active supporting commercial-discovery guide
**Owner/audience:** Product owner, founder, pilot-success owner, partnerships, sales/support leadership
**Scope:** Grade 1-10 pilot discovery. Shapes the sold slice, fee-transparency positioning, pricing hypothesis, design-partner selection, and shadow-mode conditions. It does not establish product readiness or legal compliance.
**Precedence:** Business intent remains in `../product/SCHOOLOS_BRD.md`; pilot delivery sequencing remains in `SCHOOLOS_NEXT_PHASE_DELIVERY_PLAN.md`; commercial claim language is governed by `../product/SCHOOLOS_MARKETING_AND_COMPLIANCE_CLAIMS_POLICY.md`.
**Last reviewed date:** 2026-07-01

---

**Purpose:** Run in parallel with technical hardening. Directly shapes three decisions: what goes in the sold Grade 1-10 slice, what the pricing model and positioning should be, and whether a school is a viable paid design partner.

**Sample:** 8-12 interviews across Owner/Principal, School Admin, Accountant/Cashier, Teacher, and Parent (see running order at the end).

**Do not market "guaranteed compliance."** Local-government fee approval and scholarship interpretation vary by municipality — the safe message is compliance *evidence* and operational *controls*, not legal certification. Do not let this drift in the interview itself: test whether the proposition resonates rather than selling it.

## The Proposition to Test

Read this once, in the interviewer's own words, partway through the session (after Section 1-2, before asking the reaction question in Section 3):

> "SchoolOS gives schools a clear, auditable fee and scholarship record — so approved charges, discounts, scholarships, receipts, and reversals are traceable, and parent-facing explanations are clear."

Then watch for: relief, skepticism, "we already do this," or visible discomfort. All four are useful signal. Do not defend the proposition if they push back — ask what specifically does not land.

## Section 1 — Current Fee Setup

*Ask: Principal/Owner, Admin, Accountant*

1. Walk me through your current fee headings — what do you charge for, and under what names?
2. Who decides when a fee heading changes or a new one is added?
3. How is a fee change recorded today — a register, a notice, or a verbal decision?
4. Where do parents go when they have a question or complaint about a charge?

## Section 2 — Enforcement Exposure

*Ask: Principal/Owner, Accountant*

5. Has your school had to refund a fee, respond to a complaint, or explain a charge to a local authority in the last year?
6. How are you currently tracking scholarship or discount allocations — and could you produce that record on short notice if asked?
7. If an inspector or local-level official asked for fee/scholarship records tomorrow, how long would it take you to assemble them, and would you be confident in what you found?
8. [If raised unprompted, follow up; do not introduce it first] What is your read on recent Ministry directions on admission timing and fee headings — has it changed anything day-to-day yet?

## Section 3 — Parent Trust

*Ask: Principal/Owner, Admin, Parent*

9. [Parent] When you get a fee notice or receipt, is it clear to you what it is for and how it was calculated?
10. [Parent] Have you ever been unsure whether a charge was legitimate?
11. [Principal/Admin] Do parents currently have any way to see how a scholarship or discount was applied to their child specifically?
12. **[Reaction]** Read the proposition above. What is your honest reaction?

## Section 4 — Buying Process

*Ask: Principal/Owner primarily; Accountant for budget mechanics*

13. If you were to pay for software like this, which budget line would it come from?
14. Who has to approve a new recurring cost like this — you alone, a board, or a committee?
15. Is a one-time onboarding or data-cleanup fee easier or harder to get approved than a monthly subscription?
16. Would framing this as support, training, or SMS be easier to justify internally than framing it as "software"?
17. What payment cadence actually fits how your school manages cash — annual, termly, quarterly, or monthly?
18. What would make the cost visibly worth it to you — fewer fee disputes, fewer staff hours, parent retention, audit-readiness, or something else?
19. Are you using, or have you evaluated, any other school software (Veda, Smart School, Paathshala, others)? What made you stay, leave, or not start?

## Section 5 — Diaspora Guardian

*Ask: Parent (all); Principal/Admin if they have visibility into this pattern*

20. Does another guardian — outside Nepal — manage school payments, notices, or decisions for this child? If yes, what currently breaks: making the payment, receiving notices, approving something, getting documents, or just general communication?

*Do not pitch a feature in response. Just record the pattern.*

## Section 6 — Pilot Commitment

*Ask: Principal/Owner (decision-maker), informally gauge with Admin/Accountant/Teacher*

21. Would you be willing to run attendance and fees in shadow mode — alongside your current process, not replacing it yet — for a defined period?
22. What would you personally need to see during that shadow period before trusting it to run for real?
23. What would make you stop the pilot early?
24. Realistically, who at your school would feel the most friction during a pilot, and what would you do about it?

## Suggested Running Order by Persona

| Persona | Sections | Approx. time |
|---|---|---|
| Owner/Principal | 1, 2, 3 (Q11-12), 4, 6 | 45 min |
| School Admin | 1, 3 (Q11), 5 (if visibility) | 30-40 min |
| Accountant/Cashier | 1, 2, 4 (Q13-15, 17) | 40-45 min |
| Teacher | Light touch: general awareness from Section 2-3, plus standard daily-workflow questions from the broader research plan | 30 min |
| Parent | 3 (Q9-10, 12), 5, light touch on 6 | 25-30 min |

## Synthesis Note

After the round, tag every answer against three buckets before drafting pricing or positioning:

- **(a) proposition resonated as stated**
- **(b) proposition needs reframing — note the actual words they used**
- **(c) proposition is irrelevant to their real pain**

Bucket (b) is usually the most valuable — use participants' language, not the interviewer's, in the eventual pitch.
